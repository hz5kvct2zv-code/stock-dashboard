# Nvidia Earnings Addendum - May 20, 2026

## What Nvidia Reported

Nvidia reported Q1 FY2027 results after the close on May 20, 2026:

- Revenue: $81.6B, up 85% year over year.
- Data Center revenue: $75.2B, up 92% year over year.
- GAAP EPS: $2.39.
- Non-GAAP EPS: $1.87.
- Q2 FY2027 revenue outlook: $91.0B, plus or minus 2%.
- New share repurchase authorization: $80B.
- Q2 guidance assumes no Data Center compute revenue from China.

Source: [Nvidia official earnings release](https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/nvidia-announces-financial-results-for-first-quarter-fiscal-2027).

## Why the Stock Had Little Movement

The report was fundamentally strong, but the muted stock reaction likely reflected expectations rather than weak results.

- The market already expected Nvidia to beat. A very strong print was largely the base case.
- Q2 guidance was above consensus, but not so far above the raised bar that it forced a major repricing.
- China remains an upside optionality item, not a confirmed near-term revenue contributor, because guidance assumes no China Data Center compute revenue.
- Valuation and market cap are already very large, so investors are asking whether this growth rate can persist into 2027 and 2028.
- Fed minutes kept macro pressure alive by showing officials would consider hikes if inflation stays high.
- Options/event premium likely compressed after earnings, which can make the stock look flat even when the news is good.
