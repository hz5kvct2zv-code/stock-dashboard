## Why the Market Cares
1. Nvidia reports Q1 fiscal 2027 results Wednesday, May 20. Nvidia said results are expected around 1:20 p.m. PT, with the call at 2 p.m. PT.
2. Nvidia's prior official guide was huge: Q1 FY2027 revenue expected around $78.0B plus or minus 2%, with roughly 75% gross margin, and no Data Center compute revenue from China assumed.
3. SMH is heavily exposed to Nvidia. StockAnalysis lists SMH's top holdings as NVDA 17.61%, TSM 9.70%, INTC 7.56%, AVGO 7.34%, AMD 6.86%, and MU 6.44% as of May 15.
4. The semiconductor group sold off Friday as Treasury yields spiked and investors took profit in red-hot tech. That makes Tuesday morning important: either buyers defend the dip or the group stays under pressure into earnings.
5. China remains a headline risk. Reports say U.S. approval for some H200 sales does not guarantee China will allow purchases, and no chips had shipped under that setup as of the recent report.

## Bull Case
- Nvidia remains the cleanest AI infrastructure leader.
- Hyperscaler capex is still the core support for NVDA and SMH.
- Analyst previews remain broadly positive; one cited preview expected NVDA earnings of about $1.78 on revenue near $78.98B and highlighted stronger hyperscaler capex.
- If NVDA stabilizes before earnings, SMH can recover because the ETF is concentrated in AI-chip winners.

## Bear Case
- Friday's selloff was broad across semis, not isolated.
- Higher Treasury yields pressure high-multiple AI stocks.
- NVDA earnings expectations are very high, so good results may not be enough if guidance is not exceptional.
- China/export headlines can hit sentiment quickly.
- SMH has heavy exposure to INTC, AMD, MU and other volatile names, so NVDA strength alone may not fully protect the ETF.
